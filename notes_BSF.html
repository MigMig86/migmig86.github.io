1. How can Bloch spectral functions be used to model the electronic structure of Earth-relevant minerals under extreme pressure and temperature conditions?

Inline math: \( E = mc^2 \)

Bloch spectral functions (BSFs), also denoted as

\[ A(k, \omega) \]

or Bloch spectral density, provide a momentum- (k-) and energy- (ω-) resolved description of the electronic excitations in a crystal. They are formally the imaginary part of the retarded single-particle Green's function in reciprocal space:

\[ A(k, \omega) = -\frac{1}{\pi}\,\mathrm{Im}\,\mathrm{Tr}\left[G(k, \omega)\right] \]

Another example using fractions and summation:

\[ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \]
